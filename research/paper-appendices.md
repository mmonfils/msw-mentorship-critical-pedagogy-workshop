Introduction
The purpose of this study is to explore the role of mentorship in a Master of Social Work (MSW) academic
program. This will help examine the perspectives and experiences of current MSW students and inform the
design and implementation of a mentorship program to address unmet needs. You are being invited to
participate in this study because you are a current MSW student. You should not participate ifyou are
currently under the age of 18, incarcerated, cognitively disabled, an elected official, a political candidate, an
appointed public official, or enrolled in the same research course as the principal investigator (SWGR 585
Research-Informed Practice II at Grand View University).
The interview will be conducted at Grand View University or a comparable secure setting accessible to the
general public, such as a library meeting room.
What will my participation involve?
If you agree to participate, you will be asked tocomplete an interview on your thoughts and experiences as an
MSW student. Your participation will last approximately 45 minutes. You will be asked to answer questions
about your thoughts, feelings, opinions, and experiences. Your interview will be recorded so as to ensure
accuracy in reporting your responses.
Are there any risks to me?
There are no foreseeable risks to you greater than what you would encounter in everyday life.
If you are negatively impacted at any time during or after this study, please contact the course instructor (Dr.
Cathy Beck-Cross, cbeck-cross@grandview.edu, 515-263-2969) and the Grand View Institutional Review
Board Chair (Dr. Bryan McQuide, bmcquide@grandview.edu, 515-263-2993).
Are there any benefits to me?
There are no direct benefits to you from participation in this study. Your participation will contribute to the
knowledge base on the unmet needs of MSW students, informing the design and implementation of a
mentorship program.
Compensation
You will not be compensated for participation in this study.
Explanation of Ability to Withdraw From the Research
Your participation in this study is completely voluntary. You also may refuse to answer any questions you do
not want to answer and still remain in the study. The investigator may stop the research at any time, if they
think it is best for you. You will also be informed if any changes occur during the study and if changes do
occur, you have the right to discontinue participation in this study.
